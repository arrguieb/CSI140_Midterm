Climate Crisis in the Philippines

Climate Issues

The Philippines is a country located in South Asia that is facing multiple significant climate justice issues, such as, floods, landslides, and erosion that pollutes the water in result of rising sea levels, higher temperatures, and increased frequency of typhoons and extreme weather events

Severe Flooding

Flooding is a severe issue in the Philippines, as well as other countries in the area that affects millions of people each year. Climate change has led to rising sea levels.

Typhoons

Typhoons have been having an increased frequency in South Asian countries, especially with the Philippines being ranked as the country with the highest disaster risk by the World Risk Index in 2022.

Resources

The Philippines are victim of unfair extraction of resources from its territories by large corporations, which result in the country being more vulnerable to the impacts of climate change

The Philippines' rich and natural resources provide critcal protection, offering shoreline protection, flood control, soil stability, and habitats for biodiversity.

Solutions

  • Accountability

    Even though the country's contribution to wolrd greenhouse gas emissions is small compared to the G20, studies show that the country's emissions increased by 114 percent from 1990 to 2017, largely due to the energy and industrty sectors

    The Philippines need to take steps towards climate crisis mitigation, including banning practices such as deforestation, illegal mining and logging, and plastic waste pollution, which are known to harm the environment.

  • Renewable Energy

    The USAID supports the deployment of advanced energy sector technology and works to improve the performance and resilience of energy utilities. In partnership with the Philippines' Department of Energy and Energy Regualtor Commisision, USAID is working to improve the environment for private investment and competition in the energy sector.

    This renewable energy is projected to avoid 69.7 million metric tons in carbon dioxide equivalent emissions over 15 years.

  • Natural Climate Solutions

    The USAID supports natrual climate solutions through reforestation, improved rice production technology, and improved management of livestock and livestock waste
